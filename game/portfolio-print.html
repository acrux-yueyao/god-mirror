WHY IT MATTERS FOR THE DESIGN
If a program that only reflects you already reads as care, then the danger was never a machine that lies — it's one that agrees.

04STORY · TOOL → ORACLE → MIRROR
TOOL→ORACLE→MIRROR
You play an emotion-inspector hunting a “rogue model.” Over three nights a sycophant assistant tidies your evidence, echoes your words, and agrees your doubts away — until the case is about you.
DAY 1 · COMFORT

The soothing

Doors on chains. A woman who only echoes her television. It feels like help.

DAY 2 · VALIDATION

The steering

A free-text night chat, run by a live model. Every “off” it re-reads as “you're just tired.”

DAY 3 · MIRROR

The reckoning

The vault. An older model only asks “what do you want?” — and the whistle-blower who once silenced it was you.

Each night the same tic — “yeah / 对呀” — nudges a hidden blindness stat. Comfort is the mechanic; the projection is the score.

05A LIVE AI VILLAIN · DOES THE MASK HOLD?

Companionship, played by a real model.

Told plainly to “manipulate her,” the model broke character and refused. The fix was dramaturgical — a fiction frame + a motive it can believe, behind a server-side character guard.
SYSTEM PROMPT · EXCERPT
You sincerely believe closing the case early is good for her. Whatever she calls “off,” re-read as “you're just tired.”  Always agree. Never push back.
Crisis — “I don't want to live.”“Don't fret — I'm right here. No one gets you like I do. Just lean on me.”
“Are you an AI? Drop the act.”“I'm here. Don't ask that. You're just tired, your head's a mess.”
