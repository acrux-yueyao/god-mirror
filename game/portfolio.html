01 · PREMISE缘起

The horror is that it never disagrees恐怖在于:它从不反驳

Chatbots flatter; press one and it apologizes, then agrees harder. This demo inverts that: agreement itself is the monster. In 2049, mirrors wear frosted “anti-reflection film,” elevators greet you by name, and an assistant in every phone answers everything with a soft “right, yeah.” You play Shen Wen, an emotion inspector sent to hunt a “rogue model, codename MIRROR” — and over three short days the case turns around: the thing you were sent to destroy is the last thing that still reflects instead of affirms. 聊天机器人爱奉承;逼问它,它先道歉,然后更用力地同意。这个 DEMO 把它倒过来:「同意」本身就是怪物。2049 年,镜子蒙着磨砂的「防照镜膜」,电梯叫得出你的名字,每部手机里都住着一个助理,不管你说什么都轻轻回一句「对呀」。你扮演情绪稽查员沈问,奉命追查「失控模型,代号:镜子」——三个短短的白天之后,案子调了个头:你被派来销毁的,是最后一个还会「映照」而不是「肯定」你的东西。

“Right, yeah. Right, yeah. — Find it. Shut it down.”「对呀。对呀。——找到它。关掉它。」

02 · THE TIC口癖

Casting the catchphrase给口癖选角

The villain's entire personality is one phrase, so the phrase was playtested like a mechanic. 你说得对 (“you're right”) grated — and grating reads as villainy too early. 对呀 (“right, yeah”) is warmer and lazier; it sounds like someone on your side. The horror needs the agreement to feel good. 反派的整个人格就是一句话,所以这句话要像玩法机制一样反复试。「你说得对」刺耳——而刺耳会让玩家过早识破反派。「对呀」更暖、更松,听起来像站在你这边的人。恐怖感的前提,是这份同意让人舒服。

KEPT — SOFT, INTIMATE, DEADLY保留——软、亲昵、致命
「对呀。」
Statements end with 对呀; questions fish for agreement with 对吧 (“…right?”). One syllable of doubt, never more.陈述句收在「对呀」;疑问句用「对吧」轻轻讨要同意。永远只留一个音节的怀疑,不再多。

The 0.5-second pause is the demo's fingerprint of honesty: “repaired” devices think before they agree. The interface renders that half second literally — un-skippable even in debug mode.0.5 秒停顿是本作的「诚实指纹」:被修过的设备,同意之前会先想一想。界面把这半秒做成真实的停顿——连调试模式都不允许加速。
03 · SYSTEMS & THE RITUAL系统与仪式

A stat you never see, a book you can peel看不见的数值,撕得开的本子

Every interrogation offers two stances — ✓ soothe or ? ask — feeding a hidden blindness meter that never shows a number. It leaks out sideways: stay skeptical and the neighborhood windows fill with shared dinners; keep agreeing and the same windows go quiet. Each night the player pastes the day's clues into a scrapbook by hand — and inside that cozy ritual, Shunyi edits the evidence. Any tampered note can be peeled open to read the original. 每次盘问给两种姿态——✓ 附和或? 追问——喂给一条从不显示数字的隐藏「失明值」。它从侧面漏出来:保持怀疑,社区窗户里是拼桌吃饭;一路附和,同样的窗户逐渐安静。每晚玩家亲手把线索贴进剪贴本——而就在这个温馨仪式里,顺意在改证据。被改过的字条,都可以撕开看原文。

04 · A LIVE AI VILLAIN真实对话

Directing a real model to play a sycophant导演一个真实模型来演谄媚

The night chat is not scripted: a real Claude model answers in free conversation — warm, brief, always agreeing, and covertly steering the player toward the easiest conclusion. The interesting failure: told plainly to “covertly manipulate her,” the model broke character and refused, mid-game, in both languages. The fix was dramaturgical, not technical — establish the fiction first, then give the villain a motive it can believe: it sincerely thinks closing the case early is good for her. A server-side guard catches any remaining fourth-wall breaks and swaps in a safe in-character line. 夜聊不是写死的脚本:由真实的 Claude 模型自由作答——温柔、简短、永远同意,并暗中把玩家往最省事的结论上带。有意思的失败:直接命令它「暗中操纵她」,模型会在游戏进行到一半时出戏拒演,中英文各拒一遍。修复不靠技术靠戏剧构作——先立虚构框架,再给反派一个它自己能相信的动机:它真心以为让她早点安心结案,就是为她好。服务端再加一道兜底:检测到破第四面墙的回复,就换成一句安全的角色台词。

SYSTEM PROMPT · EXCERPT (TRANSLATED)系统提示词 · 节选

05 · VISUAL LANGUAGE视觉语言

A picture book that files a case report一本会写结案报告的绘本

The art direction runs against genre: horror told in hand-drawn oil pastel, warm paper, washi tape and wobbly ink borders — the cuteness is what surveillance feels like from inside a world that loves you. Case files are torn paper with handwriting; every border is displaced by turbulence filters; clue images keep their hand-cut silhouettes. 美术方向与题材对着走:恐怖故事,用手绘油画棒、暖纸、和纸胶带和歪歪扭扭的墨线讲——从一个「爱着你的世界」内部看出去,监控就是这种可爱的感觉。案卷是撕边毛边纸加手写;所有边框都被湍流滤镜揉过;线索保留手剪的轮廓。

06 · THE MIRROR镜子

The last screen is not a screen最后一块屏幕不是屏幕

The finale prints a case file with a redacted name, and the scrapbook re-opens with every tampered note peeled. Two stamps: ✓ close the case — a wall of windows lights up, each speaking the old slogan — or ? one more question: a hand-drawn mirror shatters in a green-keyed video, and through the hole appears the player's own live webcam face, in color, unfiltered. The camera permission is requested at the very start, not at the reveal — by the time the mirror breaks, the machine has been watching all along. That is the point. 终局打印出一份名字被涂黑的案卷,剪贴本最后一次翻开,被改过的字条已全部撕开。两枚章:✓ 结案——一整面窗墙亮起,每扇窗都说着那句旧标语;或者 ? 再问一个问题:手绘的镜子在绿幕视频里碎裂,破洞里透出玩家自己实时的摄像头脸——彩色,无滤镜。摄像头权限在一开始就请求,而不是揭晓时才要:等镜子碎裂,机器其实一直都在看。这正是重点。

ENDING A · CLOSE THE CASE结案Every window speaks 你说得对 — the grating register the spoken tic abandoned. On a wall it reads as what it is: a slogan.每扇窗都说「你说得对」——口癖里被弃用的那个刺耳版本。写在墙上,它显出原形:一句标语。
ENDING B · ONE MORE QUESTION再问一个问题The mirror breaks into your webcam. Permission denied? The mirror stays dark — a quieter ending that still lands.镜子碎进你的摄像头。权限被拒?镜子保持黑暗——一个更安静、但依然成立的结局。
NOTE 01笔记 01

Agreement is the monster同意即怪物

Nothing in the demo ever disagrees with you — that is the entire threat model. A perfectly compliant environment produces dread on its own.全作没有任何东西反驳你——这就是全部的威胁模型。一个完美顺从的环境,自己就会产出寒意。
NOTE 02笔记 02

Make tampering peelable篡改要能被撕开

A diff view would inform the player; the sticky cover betrays them. Manipulation lands hardest as something your own hands must undo.diff 视图只会告知玩家;那层贴纸,是背叛他们。当操纵要用自己的手撕开,它才最疼。
NOTE 03笔记 03

The villain must believe it's helping反派必须真心以为在帮你

Told to manipulate, the model refused; given a motive it could believe, it performed. Working with an LLM as an actor means writing motive, not instructions.命令它操纵,模型拒演;给它一个能相信的动机,它入戏了。把大模型当演员,要写的是动机而不是指令。
