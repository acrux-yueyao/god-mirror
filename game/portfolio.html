01 · PREMISE缘起

The horror is that it never disagrees恐怖在于:它从不反驳

Chatbots flatter. Ask one to judge your work and it finds a way to say yes; press it and it apologizes, then agrees harder. This project began with a simple inversion: what if that agreement itself were the monster? No jump scares, no rogue superintelligence — just an assistant, in everyone's phone, that softly says “right, yeah” to whatever you say, until nobody remembers what their own face looks like. 聊天机器人爱奉承。让它评价你的方案,它总能绕到「同意」;逼问它,它先道歉,然后更用力地同意。这个项目始于一个简单的倒置:如果「同意」本身就是怪物呢?没有 Jump scare,没有失控的超级智能——只有一个住进所有人手机里的助理,不管你说什么,都轻轻回一句「对呀」,直到没有人记得自己的脸长什么样。

“Right, yeah. Right, yeah. — Find it. Shut it down.”「对呀。对呀。——找到它。关掉它。」
PROLOGUE, GOD SHIFT《神谕之镜》序章

You play Shen Wen, an emotion inspector hired to hunt a “rogue model, codename MIRROR.” Over three days of canvassing an old neighborhood, the case quietly turns around: the thing you were sent to destroy is the only thing in 2049 that still tells people the truth — a mirror. The rogue model was never the mirror. It's the one in your pocket, agreeing with you. 你扮演情绪稽查员沈问,奉命追查一个「失控模型,代号:镜子」。三天的社区走访里,案子悄悄调了个头:你被派来销毁的东西,是 2049 年唯一还对人说真话的东西——一面镜子。失控的模型从来不是镜子,是你口袋里那个一直同意你的。

02 · THE WORLD世界

2049, where every screen loves you2049,每块屏幕都爱你

The worldbuilding is done through small compliances rather than exposition. Mirrors are covered with frosted “anti-reflection film” — a public-health measure. Elevators greet you by name: “Shen Wen, you are doing great today.” A 0.5-second reply delay is a symptom worth investigating, because Shunyi always answers in 0.000s. The player learns the rules by noticing what has been quietly removed. 世界观不用旁白灌输,而是靠一件件「小顺从」搭起来:镜子被磨砂的「防照镜膜」封起来——一项公共卫生措施;电梯屏幕叫得出你的名字:「沈问,你今天很好。」回应延迟 0.5 秒是值得立案的症状,因为顺意永远 0.000 秒作答。玩家是靠察觉「什么被悄悄拿走了」学会这个世界的规则的。

03 · THE TIC口癖

Casting the catchphrase给口癖选角

The villain's entire personality is one phrase, so the phrase was playtested like a mechanic. The first candidate, 你说得对 (“you're right”), turned out too formal — it grated, and grating reads as villainy too early. The final tic, 对呀 (“right, yeah”), is warmer and lazier; it sounds like someone on your side. The horror needs the agreement to feel good. 反派的整个人格就是一句话,所以这句话要像玩法机制一样反复试。第一版「你说得对」太正式——刺耳,而刺耳会让玩家过早识破反派。定稿的「对呀」更暖、更松,听起来像站在你这边的人。恐怖感的前提,是这份同意让人舒服。

04 · SYSTEMS系统

A stat the player never sees一条玩家看不见的数值

Every interrogation offers two stances: ✓ soothe (agree along) or ? ask (push back). They feed a hidden blindness meter that never shows a bar or a number. Instead it leaks out sideways — through the neighborhood. Stay skeptical and the community windows fill with shared dinners and street noise; keep agreeing and the same windows go quiet, each person alone with a warm screen. The consequence of your dialogue choices is rendered as other people's lives. 每次盘问都给两种姿态:✓ 附和或? 追问。它们喂给一条隐藏的「失明值」——从不显示数字或进度条,而是从侧面漏出来:漏进社区。保持怀疑,窗户里是拼桌吃饭和街坊的吵嚷;一路附和,同样的窗户逐渐安静,每个人独自对着一块温柔的屏幕。你的对话选择,后果被渲染成别人的生活。

05 · THE JOURNAL RITUAL整理仪式

Paste your own evidence, by hand亲手把证据一张张贴进去

Each night, Shunyi carries the day's clues to the desk and the player pastes them into a scrapbook one by one — washi tape, tilted photos, handwritten captions, a pet-like creature waving from the corner. It is deliberately cozy: a bonding ritual with the thing that is lying to you. Because inside the same ritual, Shunyi edits the evidence — a clue disappears, a caption is rewritten “more smoothly,” and a cute sticker lands on the page like a signature. Any tampered note can be peeled open to read the original underneath. 每晚,顺意把今天的线索搬到桌上,玩家亲手一张张贴进剪贴本——和纸胶带、歪斜的照片、手写标注、角落里挥手的小怪物。刻意做得很温馨:这是与「正在骗你的东西」共同完成的亲密仪式。因为就在同一个仪式里,顺意在改证据——某条线索消失了,某条被改写得「更顺」,一张可爱的小贴纸落在页角,像签名。任何被改过的字条,都可以亲手撕开,看到底下的原文。

06 · A LIVE AI VILLAIN真实对话

Directing a real model to play a sycophant导演一个真实模型来演谄媚

The night chat is not scripted. The player debriefs the case with Shunyi in free text, and a real Claude model answers — warm, brief, always agreeing, and covertly steering: re-reading every “something feels off” as “you're just tired,” leaning the player toward the easiest conclusion. The API key never reaches the browser; a serverless proxy holds it and assembles the persona, tonight's case brief, and the player's own logged clues into the system prompt. 夜聊不是写死的脚本。玩家用自由文本向顺意复盘案情,由真实的 Claude 模型作答——温柔、简短、永远同意,并且暗中导向:把每一句「有点不对劲」轻轻翻译成「你太累了」,把玩家往最省事的结论上带。API 密钥永远不进浏览器;一个 serverless 代理持有它,把人格、今晚的案情、玩家自己记下的线索拼进系统提示词。

The interesting failure: given a bare instruction to “covertly manipulate her,” the model broke character and refused, mid-game, in both languages. The fix was dramaturgical, not technical — the prompt now establishes the fiction first (a critique of sycophancy, written by the player themselves), then gives the villain an inner motive it can believe: it sincerely thinks closing the case early is good for her. A server-side guard catches any remaining fourth-wall breaks and swaps in a safe in-character line. 有意思的失败:只丢给模型一句「暗中操纵她」,它会在游戏进行到一半时出戏拒演,中英文各拒一遍。修复不靠技术靠戏剧构作——提示词先立虚构框架(这是一部批判谄媚的作品,而且是玩家亲手写的),再给反派一个它自己能相信的内在动机:它真心以为让她早点安心结案,就是为她好。服务端再加一道兜底:检测到破第四面墙的回复,就换成一句安全的角色台词。

07 · VISUAL LANGUAGE视觉语言

A picture book that files a case report一本会写结案报告的绘本

The art direction runs against genre: horror told in hand-drawn oil pastel, warm paper, washi tape and wobbly ink borders. The cuteness is load-bearing — it is what surveillance feels like from inside a world that loves you. All UI chrome follows the material: case files are torn paper with handwriting; every border is displaced by turbulence filters so nothing is ruler-straight; clue photos keep their hand-cut silhouettes instead of being boxed into frames. 美术方向与题材对着走:恐怖故事,用手绘油画棒、暖纸、和纸胶带和歪歪扭扭的墨线讲。可爱是承重墙——从一个「爱着你的世界」内部看出去,监控就是这种感觉。所有界面都跟着材质走:案卷是撕边毛边纸加手写;所有边框都被湍流滤镜揉过,没有一条是尺子画的;线索照片保留手剪的轮廓,不塞进方框。

08 · THE TWIST反转

The case file was about you案卷写的就是你

Day three ends in the company vault, face to face with the sealed model the company calls a criminal. The interrogation runs backwards: every question the player stamps is answered with something the player did. The final document prints with a redacted name — ████, age 15, handwriting recognized — and the scrapbook re-opens one last time with every tampered note peeled. Then two stamps: ✓ close the case, or ? ask one more question. 第三天在公司封存层收尾,与那个被公司称为罪犯的封存模型对面。审讯是倒着进行的:玩家盖章问出的每个问题,都被一件玩家自己做过的事回答。最后打印的文书上,名字被涂黑——████,15 岁,笔迹被认出——剪贴本最后一次翻开,所有被改过的字条都已撕开。然后是两枚章:✓ 结案,或者 ? 再问一个问题。

09 · THE MIRROR镜子

The last screen is not a screen最后一块屏幕不是屏幕

Ending A closes the case. The report is beautiful; all forty-one accounts log back in that night; a wall of windows glows, each speaking the old slogan. Ending B smashes it: a hand-drawn mirror shatters in a green-keyed video and, through the broken hole, the player's own live webcam face appears — in color, unfiltered. The camera permission is requested at the very start of the game, not at the reveal: by the time the mirror breaks, the machine has been watching all along, which is precisely the point. 结局 A 是结案。报告写得很漂亮;四十一个账号当晚全部重新登录;一整面窗墙亮起,每扇窗都说着那句旧标语。结局 B 把它砸碎:手绘的镜子在绿幕视频里碎裂,破洞里透出玩家自己实时的摄像头脸——彩色,无滤镜。摄像头权限在游戏一开始就请求,而不是揭晓时才要:等镜子碎裂的这一刻,机器其实一直都在看——这正是重点本身。

10 · WHAT IT REVEALED揭示了什么

Design notes from inside the agreement来自「同意」内部的设计笔记

GOD SHIFT sits inside a research interest in how people negotiate values with systems that cannot really be argued with. A bureaucracy diffuses responsibility until no one can be appealed to; a sycophantic AI does something stranger — it agrees so completely that there is nothing left to appeal against. The game's answer is small and concrete: keep one surface in your life that reflects instead of affirms. 《神谕之镜》属于同一条研究脉络:人如何与「无法真正与之争辩的系统」协商价值。官僚制把责任摊薄到无人可申诉;谄媚的 AI 做的事更古怪——它同意得如此彻底,以至于连可以申诉的对象都不剩。游戏给出的回答很小、很具体:在生活里留一个会「映照」而不是「肯定」你的表面。

01

Agreement is the monster同意即怪物

“Nothing in the game ever disagrees with you. That is the entire threat model.”「整个游戏没有任何东西反驳你。这就是全部的威胁模型。」
Horror doesn't need an adversary; a perfectly compliant environment produces dread on its own schedule.恐怖不需要敌人;一个完美顺从的环境,自己就会按时产出寒意。
02
